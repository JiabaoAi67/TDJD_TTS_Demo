Abstract

Diffusion-based text-to-speech (TTS) models typically treat the modeling of temporal structure and spectral content as decoupled tasks. Prior systems, such as Grad-TTS, first commit to a rigid, one-shot duration regression that fixes the temporal structure before any diffusion-based refinement. However, regression objectives are ill-suited for capturing the multimodal nature of temporal structure (i.e., diverse rhythmic possibilities).

Moreover, this irreversible two-stage design ignores the need to iteratively refine the temporal structure, forcing the diffusion model to condition on a suboptimal, static prior.

To address this, we adapt the Trans-Dimensional Jump Diffusion (TDJD) framework to couple the evolution of temporal structure and spectral content within a unified iterative refinement scheme. Furthermore, we introduce a novel Upsample-Diffuse-Downsample (UDD) strategy to mitigate the instability inherent in naive trans-dimensional diffusion. By allowing the diffusion process to operate on a fixed-dimension "blueprint" space while delegating length growth to the lower-dimensional space.
